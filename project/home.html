What is a Greenhouse?

Greenhouse Description

A greenhouse is a structure that is designed to regulate the temperature and humidity of the environment inside. There are different types of greenhouses, but they all have large areas covered with transparent materials that let sunlight pass and block it as heat.(1)

Greenhouse Importance

Offer a controlled environment for plant growth, allowing for increased production, extended growing seasons, and protection from pests and extreme weather. They also provide an optimal environment for starting seeds and growing a wider variety of plants than would be inpossible in outdoors, this cualitys help for a production all the year in the wold and for places whit dificult clime to have food on a save and sustainable way.(2)

Greenhouse Information

Materials: For structural pilars: steel, plastic or wood, for walls and roofs: rigid plastic, plastic film, or glass panes.(1)

Functionality: The warmer temperature inside is because the solar radiation passes through the transparent roof and walls and is absorbed by the elements inside and become warmer. These in turn warm up the surrounding air, the structure is not open, the warmed air cannot escape, so the temperature inside rises.(1)

Ventilation: One of the most important components in a successful greenhouse, the main purpose is regulate the temperature and humidity to the optimal level.(1)

Tecnology: For a structure be considared a grenhose only needs fulfit the warmer temperature, tecnaly no nesesary any tipe of electricity, this tipe of greenhouses are caled of low tech, medium tech have some tings automatizade, like the irrigation and high tech hare almost completely automated, even the clime inside.(1)

Plants: Are often used for growing flowers, vegetables, fruits, and transplants. Special varieties of certain crops, such as tomatoes, are generally used for commercial production.(1)

Greenhouse Mainteined

Maintaining a greenhouse involves routine tasks like cleaning the structure to maximize light exposure, managing pests and diseases, and optimizing ventilation and air circulation to prevent problems. Regular inspections of frames, glazing, heating, and ventilation systems are also crucial. Proper horticultural practices, including removing dead plants and debris.(3)
